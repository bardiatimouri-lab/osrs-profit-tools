❓ How this works & the strategy (read me)
  • High Level Alchemy (level 55 Magic, free-to-play) turns an item into gold equal to its high alch value, using 1 nature rune (+5 fire runes, or 0 with a Staff of Fire).
  • Profit per alch = High alch value − Buy price − Rune cost. No GE tax applies — alching gives coins directly.
  • "Pay up to" (break-even) = High alch − Rune cost. If you buy below this, you profit. Buy above it and you lose money. This is the single number to memorise per item.
  • Liquidity is everything. An item showing huge profit but only a handful of daily trades cannot actually be bought. This app ranks real, buyable items — that's why high-volume rune armour usually wins.
  • GE buy limits cap how many of one item you can buy every 4 hours. The buy plan respects them, so you spread gold across several items.
  • Your gold recycles. Each alch returns ~99% of what you paid plus the profit, so after a batch you have your gold back and can buy again. The 4-hour limits, not your gold, are usually the real cap — repeat the plan each cycle.
  • Tip: "Patient offer" pricing buys cheaper (more profit) but fills slower. "Instant buy" is the realistic cost to grab items right now.
  • Flipping = buy an item at the low (insta-sell) price, then resell at the high (insta-buy) price, pocketing the spread. It's AFK — you place offers and walk away, no clicking like alching.
  • Net profit = Sell price − Buy price − 2% GE tax (the tax is taken on the sale). The app already subtracts it, so "Net/item" is your real profit.
  • The grade (A–F) is the whole story. Each flip is scored on four things: is the price steady or rising (not falling), how fast it fills, how deep the market is, and how stable the margin is. A/B = buy it. C = only if you're paying attention. D/F = don't.
  • 🛡️ The falling-knife rule (why this is different). A buy offer fills fastest exactly when the price is dropping — sellers dump into your bid, then it keeps falling and your resale target is gone. So the tool refuses to recommend anything trending down, no matter how fat the margin looks. Open "Refused N flips" under the plan to see every one it skipped and why.
  • Fast beats fat. The plan prefers a small margin that round-trips in minutes over a big margin that takes hours — because the longer your gold sits in an offer, the more the price can move against you. "Fills in" shows the estimate.
  • It's not guaranteed like alching. Prices drift and offers sit. The plan diversifies (max 40% on one item) and the profit shown is deliberately risk-adjusted (trend + volatility discounted) so it under-promises.
  • Flip mode: Safe / Balanced / All. Safe (default) shows only A/B, refuses any downtrend, and demands deep, fast markets — start here. Balanced adds C. All shows everything for browsing (the plan still only buys A/B).
  • Item universe (F2P / Members / All): F2P items are cheap, so they can only absorb a few million of capital. Members items are higher-value with fatter margins and soak up big bankrolls — switch to Members if you're a member with lots of gold and the plan says your capital is under-used.
  • Smart margin (why you can trust the numbers): every flip is checked against its typical 24-hour margin. If today's spread is much wider than usual (⚡) it may be a temporary blip that closes before you sell, so the plan quietly uses the smaller, reliable margin. Flukes (⚠ — items that normally have no margin) are kept out of the plan entirely. The result: the plan under-promises and over-delivers instead of chasing mirages.
  • Max price per item caps how much gold sits on a single flip — lower = safer and fills faster. Combined with the plan's built-in diversification (no flip > 40% of your bankroll), you're never all-in on one item.
